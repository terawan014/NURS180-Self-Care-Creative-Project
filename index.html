Daily Poetry
Vol. 001

Beauty in becoming

There is a kind of healing
that does not arrive loudly.
It enters with warm tea,
a slower breath,
a notebook opened after midnight.
This semester taught me
that self-care is not escape,
but a return.

A return to my body
when stress asked too much of it.
A return to language
when my thoughts felt crowded.
A return to morning light,
to stretching, walking,
to meals made with attention,
to quiet that did not feel empty.

I wrote poems in fragments,
between deadlines and long weeks,
and each line became proof
that care can be small
and still be real.
A glass of water.
A window opened.
A page that held what I could not say aloud.

I am learning that resilience
is not the absence of strain.
It is the choice to soften,
to pause before breaking,
to keep making space
for beauty, even here.

And in that space,
I found myself again.

Semester Archive

Evidence of ongoing self-care through poetry

These short entries reflect the main stressors, coping strategies, and course reflections that shaped my self-care practice this semester.

January 9, 2026

Academic pressure

This term began with pressure.
Upper year courses felt heavier,
and I kept thinking about grades,
deadlines, and the future.
Writing helped me admit that stress
was already part of my daily life.

January 21, 2026

Changing my coping habits

Watching shows and listening to music
can calm me down for a while,
but they do not always solve the problem.
This was when I started thinking about
smaller routines, better planning,
and rest that felt more intentional.

February 24, 2026

Supporting diversity

This reflection reminded me
that stress is not only personal.
Discrimination and microaggressions
affect mental health too.
Caring for myself also means learning
how to help create a safer community.

March 10, 2026

Digital detox

I noticed how often I reached
for short videos during study breaks.
Stepping away for a few hours
made me feel calmer and less distracted.
It showed me that focus can return
when I protect my attention.

March 22, 2026

Writing as self-care

Poetry became a quiet way
to reflect on stress without avoiding it.
It helped me slow down,
organize my thoughts,
and notice that self-care does not need
to be dramatic to be meaningful.

Focus Areas

Self-care themes explored

  • Psychological and physiological responses to academic stress
  • Resilience through reflection, routine, and gradual behavior change
  • Stress-reducing strategies including music, writing, rest, and digital boundaries
  • Physical, mental, and emotional self-care through planning, recovery, and mindfulness
  • Sociocultural awareness, diversity, and the role of supportive community care
